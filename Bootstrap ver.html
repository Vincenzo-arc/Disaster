Introduction to Disaster Preparedness

Disaster preparedness involves planning and taking steps to ensure safety before, during, and after emergencies. It includes understanding potential risks, creating emergency plans, assembling disaster kits, and educating yourself and others about safety measures. Being prepared can significantly reduce panic and confusion, ultimately saving lives and minimizing damage during a disaster.

Safety Tips for Earthquakes

Before the disaster

Here are some essential safety tips to follow before an earthquake:

  • Secure Heavy Items: Anchor heavy furniture, appliances, and shelving to walls to prevent them from tipping over.
  • Create a Family Emergency Plan: Discuss and practice an emergency plan with your family. Identify safe spots in your home and establish meeting places outside.
  • Prepare a Disaster Kit: Assemble a kit with essentials such as:
    • Water (one gallon per person per day for at least three days)
    • Non-perishable food (at least a three-day supply)
    • Flashlight and extra batteries
    • First aid kit
    • Whistle (to signal for help)
    • Dust mask (to help filter contaminated air)
    • Local maps
  • Know Your Risk: Understand the seismic risk in your area and stay informed about local earthquake preparedness resources.
  • Practice Drills: Conduct regular earthquake drills with your family to ensure everyone knows what to do when shaking starts.
  • Educate Yourself: Learn about earthquake safety tips and the best protective measures, such as "Drop, Cover, and Hold On."
  • Check Insurance Coverage: Review your insurance policies to ensure you have adequate coverage for earthquake damage.

During the disaster

  1. Drop, Cover, and Hold On: Get down on your hands and knees, cover your head and neck under sturdy furniture, and hold on until the shaking stops.
  2. Stay Indoors: If you are inside, remain there. Stay away from windows, glass, and heavy objects that could fall.
  3. Stay Put: If you are in bed, stay there and protect your head with a pillow.
  4. Avoid Doorways: Contrary to popular belief, doorways are not the safest place. Find cover under furniture instead.
  5. If Outside: Move to an open area away from buildings, trees, and utility wires. Stay there until the shaking stops.
  6. If Driving: Pull over to a clear area, stop, and stay inside your vehicle until the shaking stops.
  7. If in a Crowded Place: Stay calm, remain where you are, and avoid rushing for exits.
  8. Be Prepared for Aftershocks: Aftershocks often occur following the main earthquake. Be ready to drop, cover, and hold on again.

After the disaster

  • Check for Injuries: Assess yourself and others for injuries. Provide first aid as necessary.
  • Stay Informed: Listen to local authorities for updates via radio, TV, or mobile alerts.
  • Avoid Damaged Areas: Stay away from buildings, bridges, and roads that may be at risk of collapse.
  • Inspect Your Home: Check for gas leaks, water leaks, and electrical issues. If you smell gas, evacuate immediately.
  • Use Caution When Cleaning Up: Wear sturdy shoes and gloves while cleaning up debris.
  • Be Prepared for Aftershocks: Aftershocks can occur after the main quake. Stay alert.
  • Communicate: Use text messages or social media to contact loved ones, as phone lines may be overloaded.
  • Document Damage: Take photos of any damage to your property for insurance purposes.
